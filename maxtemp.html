Unsurprisingly, the data does show that a city's daily maximum temperature is strongly correlated with latitude. Solar energy has to travel through more atmosphere at the poles, and less of it reaches the surface of the earth. At the time of the analysis, it was summer in the Northern Hemisphere, so the maximum temperatures were clustered closer to the Tropic of Cancer than the equator.
